About

Currently enrolled in Video Game Development at triOS College

Currently in my studies I've learned Unity, C++, and a little insider stuff about game development in larger studios. I'm excited to get into game development more as I'm really passionate about video games, I've been playing them for so many years now that I feel like it wasn't even a question if I should go into this industry or not. Honestly, I feel that I've always had good ideas but its been in execution where I've struggled the most, and I'm hoping that my time here at Eastern College will help me to achieve that goal of getting my ideas out there to a wider audience. My biggest goal is to make a game that leaves as much of an impact on people and is as special as Undertale is to me.

Video Games to me has never been a medium primarily just for making money, I see it as a form of art. I've got the mindset that if you are not passionate about the games you are creating then you are destined to burn out, and the overall feel of the game will suffer. See its not just a form of making money, its a medium of telling stories, letting other people experience ideas and worlds that you can actually interact with. Unlike movies and books, Video Games can put you directly into the worlds that Video Game Designers create for us. I want to leave a lasting impact on anybody who plays my games, to look back at them as not necessarily happy nor sad, but with fondness.

I'm passionate about the video games I play

Project Stargame

The stars each represent a human soul lost from Earth, one day they start to disappear from the sky so our protagonist decides to go into the “Spirit Realm”, a dimension stuck between sleep and death where the souls of fallen stars reside. The protagonist’s job is to figure out what happened to those fallen stars and to put them back into the sky, along the way you will meet new characters that you will (should) grow to love, but eventually you’ll have to set spirits free, and lay their souls to rest

Coronal Wasteland

Earth gets struck by a Coronal Mass Ejection (CME) which caused all systems running on electricity to cease functionality at an instant. All Military, Medical, quality of life, food and water, factories, basically anything that is ran on electricity gone at a snap of a finger, and by the time they would have been repaired society has already collapsed in on itself. Chaos ensues as humanity seemingly grinds to a halt. Gangs of Bandits roam about killing as many innocent people as they can, stealing from the weak for their own personal gain, settlements of innocent people are sparse to say the least, and with very little resources life is harsh day in and day out. Abandoned cities become the norm as life becomes survival of the fittest on this kill or be killed desolate wasteland also known as the Earth.

Project Sad Game

You play as a man who commits suicide in the opening of the game. The entire game depicts the unfortunate events leading up to this point as if the man’s entire life is flashing right before his eyes. The game would start off nice and cheery and as time goes on the colors of each level would become less and less saturated and the character would see more hallucinations as the man is devoured by the loss of loved ones, depression, drug and alcohol abuse. Not knowing how to cope with all his struggles the game ends with his final dying moments, the game then switches to third person as the man’s life leaves his eyes and his body is left lifeless.
